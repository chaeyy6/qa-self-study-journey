# Story of the Requirements

## Learning Objective

Understand how software projects begin, how requirements flow between stakeholders, and how QA Engineers contribute before development starts.

---

## Key Concepts

- Client
- Product Owner (PO)
- Requirements
- Developers
- QA Engineers
- Early Testing

---

## My Understanding

This lesson helped me understand that every software project begins with gathering business requirements. The client communicates their needs to the Product Owner or Business Analyst, who documents those requirements before sharing them with both the development and QA teams.

I also learned that QA Engineers do not simply wait for developers to finish coding. Instead, they review the requirements, identify ambiguities, ask clarification questions, and begin planning how the application will be tested. This allows potential issues to be identified much earlier in the development process.

---

## QA Perspective

As a future QA Engineer, I learned that understanding the requirements is just as important as testing the finished application. Before development even begins, I should review the requirements carefully, ask questions whenever something is unclear, and ensure that the feature can be tested effectively. This early involvement helps improve software quality and reduces misunderstandings during development.

---

## Real-World Example

### Requirement

> "Users shall be able to log in using their email and password."

During requirement review, a QA Engineer might ask:

- What happens after five consecutive failed login attempts?
- Should the email remain after the user logs out?
- Are passwords case-sensitive?
- What validation rules should the email field follow?

Clarifying these questions before development helps ensure that both developers and testers have the same understanding of the feature.

## Interview Questions

- What is a software requirement?
- What is the role of a Product Owner?
- Why is requirement analysis important in software testing?
- What is Early Testing?
- What should a QA Engineer do if a requirement is unclear?

---

## Key Takeaways

- Every software project begins with gathering business requirements.
- Requirements guide both developers and QA Engineers.
- QA Engineers participate early by reviewing requirements and asking clarification questions.
- Identifying issues before development saves time and reduces the cost of fixing defects later.
