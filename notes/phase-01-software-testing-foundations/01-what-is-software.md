# What is Software?

## Learning Objective

Develop a foundational understanding of what software is and recognize that software can be found in many aspects of everyday life and technology.

---

## Key Concepts

- Software
- Software Domains
- Specialized QA Roles

---

## My Understanding

Before this lesson, I mostly associated software with websites and mobile applications. After studying the topic, I realized that software is much broader than that. It powers operating systems, video games, artificial intelligence, embedded devices, vehicles, and many other technologies that people use daily.

Since software serves different purposes across industries, the responsibilities and knowledge required of a QA Engineer may also vary depending on the product being tested.

---

## QA Perspective

Understanding the type of software being tested is important because each domain presents different challenges, user expectations, and testing priorities. A QA Engineer should first understand the product before deciding how to approach testing.

---

## Real-World Examples

- Social media platforms
- Mobile banking applications
- Video games
- AI-powered assistants
- Vehicle infotainment systems
- Embedded devices

---

## Interview Questions

- How would you define software?
- What are some examples of software in different industries?
- Why is it important for QA Engineers to understand the software domain they are testing?

---

## Key Takeaways

- Software extends beyond websites and mobile applications.
- Different industries develop different types of software.
- QA Engineers often need domain knowledge to test software effectively.
- Understanding the product is one of the first steps toward creating an effective testing strategy.
