# Scrum Framework

## Learning Objective

Understand the Scrum Framework, its workflow, and how Agile teams organize work into short iterations called Sprints to continuously deliver valuable software.

## My Understanding

Scrum is an Agile framework that breaks a large software project into smaller, manageable iterations called Sprints. Instead of waiting until the end of development to release the entire application, Scrum focuses on delivering small but usable pieces of the product at regular intervals.

The development process begins with a product vision. From that vision, the Product Owner creates and prioritizes a Product Backlog containing User Stories and other work items. During Sprint Planning, the team selects a realistic amount of work to complete within the Sprint. These selected items become the Sprint Backlog.

Throughout the Sprint, developers, testers, and other team members collaborate to design, build, test, and deliver a working product increment. Daily Standup meetings help the team stay aligned by discussing completed work, upcoming tasks, and blockers.

Scrum also emphasizes flexibility. Instead of fixing all requirements at the beginning of a project, Agile fixes the available time and team resources while allowing requirements to evolve as new information and customer feedback become available.

---

## QA Perspective

As a QA Engineer, Scrum means that testing is performed continuously throughout each Sprint instead of only after all development has finished. QA Engineers collaborate closely with developers, Product Owners, and other team members to prepare test scenarios, execute testing, report defects, and verify completed User Stories before the Sprint ends.

Understanding the Product Backlog and Sprint Backlog also helps QA Engineers know which features should be tested during the current Sprint.

## Real-World Example

Imagine a team developing an online bookstore.

Instead of building every feature before releasing the application, the work is divided into multiple Sprints.

Sprint 1
- User Registration
- Login

Sprint 2
- Browse Books
- Search

Sprint 3
- Shopping Cart

Sprint 4
- Checkout

At the end of every Sprint, the team delivers a working portion of the application, gathers feedback, and plans the next Sprint.

## Key Takeaways

- Scrum divides large projects into smaller, manageable Sprints.
- The Product Backlog contains all planned work for the product.
- The Sprint Backlog contains only the work selected for the current Sprint.
- Scrum promotes continuous collaboration, testing, and delivery.
- Agile adapts to changing requirements while keeping Sprint duration and team size consistent.
- Delivering small, working increments reduces project risk and allows teams to gather feedback early.
