# Sequential, Iterative & Incremental Development

## Learning Objective

Understand the different Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) models and how QA Engineers contribute throughout each development approach.

---

## Key Concepts

- Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)
- Sequential Development
- Waterfall Model
- V-Model
- Agile Development
- Iterative Development
- Incremental Development
- Early Testing

---

## My Understanding

This lesson introduced me to the different approaches used to develop software. I learned that Sequential Development follows a structured, linear process where each phase is completed before moving to the next. Two common examples are the Waterfall Model and the V-Model.

I also learned that the V-Model encourages QA Engineers to become involved early by reviewing requirements and preparing testing activities before development is completed. This concept, known as Early Testing, helps identify issues before code is written.

In contrast, Agile Development delivers software in smaller pieces over multiple iterations. This allows teams to receive feedback frequently, adapt to changing requirements, and continuously improve the product throughout development.

---

## QA Perspective

As a future QA Engineer, understanding SDLC models helps me recognize when I should contribute during software development. Instead of waiting until coding is finished, I can participate earlier by reviewing requirements, planning test scenarios, and preparing test cases. Knowing whether a project follows Waterfall, the V-Model, or Agile also helps me understand how testing activities fit into the overall development process.

---

## Real-World Examples

### Waterfall Development

A government payroll system where all requirements are finalized before development begins and changes are kept to a minimum.

### V-Model

A healthcare application where requirements are reviewed carefully and testing activities are planned early to meet strict quality standards.

### Agile Development

An e-commerce platform where new features, promotions, and improvements are released regularly through short development iterations.

## Key Takeaways

- SDLC provides a structured process for developing software.
- Sequential Development follows a linear approach, while Agile delivers software incrementally through multiple iterations.
- The V-Model encourages QA Engineers to participate early by reviewing requirements and planning testing activities.
- Understanding SDLC models helps QA Engineers know when and how to contribute during software development.
